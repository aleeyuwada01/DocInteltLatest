The Architect's Glossary

To execute this level of mathematics, you must deeply understand every gear inside the engine. This is the Field Guide.

Part I: The Pyramiding Engine

This simulator measures Intra-Trade Compounding — using floating profit as margin to build a massive position during a single unidirectional market move.

Input Variables

  • Initial Capital: The seed money risked on the very first entry. Should be an amount you are completely detached from.
  • Entry Price: The exact market level where your "Primer Entry" is executed.
  • Price Step: The distance the market must move in your favor before executing the next stack.
  • Profit per Level: The raw dollar value your base lot size generates per Price Step.
  • Stack Levels: The number of consecutive times you add a new position to the winning trade.

Output Metrics

  • Static Final (1 Lot): The "Retail Baseline." Linear growth from holding the original position.
  • Stacked Final: The "Miracle Result." Final equity from relentless pyramiding.
  • Miracle Multiplier: How many times more profit stacking generated vs. a standard hold.
  • Final Lots Active: The combined weight of your position at close.

Part II: The Micro-Sprint Engine

This simulator measures Sequential Compounding — rolling the total balance of each closed winner into the next trade's risk calculation.

Input Variables

  • Starting Balance: The absolute maximum you can lose if Trade 1 fails.
  • Trade Window: The maximum trades you allow before withdrawing. Your defense against the Probability of Ruin.
  • Win Rate: Your probability of a setup hitting TP before SL.
  • R:R Ratio: For every $1 risked, how much you demand in return.
  • Risk per Trade: The velocity of the compound — the % of your compounded balance risked per trade.

Output Metrics

  • Final Balance: What you walk away with.
  • Peak Balance: The highest equity point before any losses.
  • Max Drawdown: The largest peak-to-trough drop. The Psychological Pain Metric.
  • Max Loss Streak: Highest consecutive losses encountered.

✦ The Golden Sweet Spots

1. Pyramiding Sweet Spot (VIX 75 / Gold M1)

You are looking for an absolute free-fall or a parabolic squeeze.

  • Initial Capital: Very Low ($50–$100).
  • Price Step: Microscopic (M1/M5 timeframes).
  • Stack Levels: 4 to 6. A 10-level stack guarantees getting caught in a pullback. 4–6 captures the meat of a momentum burst.
  • Golden Rule: Move your SL to BE/profit on older trades the exact second a new stack is placed.

2. Micro-Sprint Sweet Spot (High-Risk Sniper)

Explode an account over a week — not a single day.

  • Trade Window: 8–12 Trades. Past 12 invites the Law of Large Numbers.
  • Win Rate: 60–65%.
  • R:R Ratio: 2.0+ (For 1:10 Sniper setups, reduce to 3–5 trades).
  • Risk per Trade: 15–20%. Over 25% is a coin flip. 15–20% provides violent upside while surviving a 2-trade losing streak.
